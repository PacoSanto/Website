About Me

Francesco Santoro was born in 1994, but his growth was tragically interrupted at the age of 16, when he realized he wanted to devote himself to studying rather than to his two other youthful passions: karate and saxophone. Considered himself a "complete time waster" until the age of 21, Francesco then decided to apply his talent for geology in the direction of mineralogy. His work includes a Marie Curie doctorate still ongoing at the University of Granada and various social participations for representation from high school to doctorate, the possibility of having a thousand passions including music, climbing, meditative practices, combat sports and padel but not being able to finish even one of them, but rediscovering them sporadically from time to time. Lives around Europe based in Granada. Stubborn in following his bizarre ideas such as pushing to live near a sea to someday learn to fish, reviving the cult of calcite and its pillars, and creating scientific articles on stabilizing amorphous phases of prenucleation. He is trying to add his mother's family surname to his name, because it is nicer to read in the articles and more respectful to the family, but he is too lazy to do the paperwork and has only the surname on the scientific articles for now. He is in love with the cult of the International of Milano and considers the discovery of pasta as the greatest leap in human progress until the invention of the Internet. Many people see him not just as a geologist but more as a building materials expert living in Granada for work, but his neighbors wish he didn't because of playing vinyls with peculiar tastes.
